WHAT DOES AN HRU DO?

If the vessel sinks, the increasing water pressure activates the Hammar H20. It automatically releases the liferafts, EPIRBs (Emergency Position Indicating Radio Beacons), or other life-saving appliances it is connected to.

The Hammar H20 is designed to be safe and reliable in all extreme conditions that you and your ship might meet on the oceans. The Manual Release Units (MRU) and Electronic Release Units (ERU) allow for a smooth, controlled release in an early stage of abandoning the ship without compromising the hydrostatic function.

 

Read more

A lashed liferaft will follow the ship down if no mechanism is there to release the lashing; this is the purpose of an HRU. All vessels complying with the SOLAS (Safety for Life at Sea) regulation are obliged to have HRUs on sufficient liferafts for saving the personnel onboard. Not only will the SOLAS HRU release the liferaft, but the Weak Link™ connected to the raft's painter line also activates the liferaft after release, making it available to survivors to board.

The Hammar H20 White Strong Rope withstands forces up to 15kN, and the HRU should release at a depth of between 1,5 to 4 meters. Once the liferaft is set free, the Weak Link™ should hold the painter line to the force of 2,2kN (+/- 0,4kN).

SOLAS defines two different types of HRUs:

  • Non-disposable, which require service yearly and are generally made of metal.
  • Disposable, which require no maintenance during its lifetime of 24 or 30 months, such as the Hammar H20.

THE HAMMAR H20 - HOW IT WORKS

Inside the Hammar H20, a pin under tension sits in a notch of a spring-loaded knife. The pin is attached to the center of a flexible membrane. At the intended depth, water acts on the membrane pushing it inwards and out of the notch. When the pin is fully out of the notch, the blade can instantly spring forward, cutting through the strong rope and releasing the lashing.

The Weak Link™ holds the painter line assuring the necessary pull to inflate the raft as the buoyancy of the liferaft container floats towards the surface. The increased buoyancy of the inflated liferaft will cause the Weak Link™ to break, and the raft can surface.

The Hammar H20 is designed to cut through the White Strong Rope but may, in some applications, also cut the unshielded electrical cable.

MRU and ERU

HOW AN MRU AND ERU WORKS

The MRU and ERU are release units in Remote Release Systems (RRS). The MRU and the ERU work on the same principle as the HRU. In addition to their pneumatic or the electric option of release, they will activate under hydrostatic pressure.

Manual Release Unit (MRU)

The MRU may be released using pneumatics. A pump creates a vacuum in the compartment over the membrane lifting the pin holding the spring-loaded knife. An MRU will activate automatically under about four meters of hydrostatic pressure.

Electronic Release Unit (ERU)

The electronic signal sent to an ERU is received by an actuator creating an instant lifting force on the membrane, lifting the pin, and releasing the blade. ERUs are available in three different hydrostatic activation depths; 1,5 to 4 meters, 4 meters, or 6 meters.

DUAL ASSEMBLY

The H20 Dual Assembly allows for more than one release option and/or redundancy, or dual release points. This is achieved by connecting MRUs and ERUs to an HRU. This way, you can fulfill SOLAS requirements combined with RRS.

Installation

Dual Arrangement is supplied in various shapes and forms for installing. A few points are important for the correct function of the unit:

  • In arrangements with two connections to a strong point (deck), these connection points need to be separated by 60 to 120 millimeters.
  • The Weak Link™ may be either a red rope or a Weak Link™ system, depending on the arrangement. Confirm the Weak Link™ is the only thing holding the painter line after an automatic activation.
  • Confirm the joint between the two rope slings is outside the stainless steel ring to avoid pinching upon release.

H20 R FOR RAFTS

H20 for Rafts

H20 FOR RAFTS

The Hammar H20 is an HRU designed to fit liferafts of all shapes and sizes. It is made of glass fiber reinforced nylon, which prevents it from corroding. The H20 needs no annual service, maintenance, or spare parts. You simply install a new unit and dispose of the old once it has reached the end of service life.

The Hammar H20 White Strong Rope is secured to the deck or liferaft cradle and attached to the liferaft lashing with a slip hook. If the ship sinks, the water pressure will, within four meters, activate the sharp knife, which cuts the rope, and the liferaft will float free. Lifetime

The H20 is available in a 24 month (H20 R) and a 30 month (H20 RX) operational life option. An H20 may be stored for up to five years (shelf life) from manufacturing before installed onboard. It should be stored in its box in a dry, temperature regulated, and UV-protected environment.

Approvals

The SOLAS 74/96 Convention states that life-saving appliances should comply with the LSA (Life-Saving Appliances) Code's applicable requirements (as amended by IMO Resolution MSC. 48(66)). LSA is subject to General Requirements in LSA-Code ch 1.2. You will find requirements such as that the product should not be damaged in air temperatures in the range of -30°C to +65°C and that it should be resistant to corrosion and deterioration when exposed to sunlight. The requirements for Float Free Arrangements for Liferafts are found in the LSA Code in chapter 4.1.6 and Hydrostatic Release Unit in chapter 4.1.6.3. All Hammar H20 R are suitable for all SOLAS liferafts, even those with a capacity of over 25 persons.

All Hammar H20 units are approved all over the world and fulfill the requirements of:

H20 FOR EPIRBS

The Hammar H20 is widely used for EPIRBs. Fifty percent of the world's EPIRB manufacturers are using the Hammar H20 for the free float function. If the vessel sinks, the Hammar H20 is activated by increasing water pressure and automatically releases the EPIRB. When reaching the surface, the EPIRB starts to emit a signal of identification and position to a satellite. Instead of the rope sling, a specially developed plastic bolt secures the radio beacon and will be cut when the unit is activated.

Approvals

Free-float arrangements for emergency radio equipment are regulated in Resolution A.662(16), and the approval to SOLAS lies with the manufacturer of the EPIRB and bracket. This differs a lot from the H20 for rafts, where Hammar has a type approval and under the European Commission receives a "wheelhouse" mark. For free float arrangements of liferafts, a whole section in Resolution MSC. 48(66) is dedicated to the performance and construction of the HRU for rafts.

Bolts/Rods

Hammar produces the release mechanism for many of the world's EPIRB manufacturers. The EPIRB manufacturers, in many cases, produce their own bolts, and the H20 E is regarded as a component in the float-free bracket.

Important

Yellow, Green, or Blue?

The Hammar H20 is available in three different activation depths: 1,5 to 4 meters (YELLOW), 4 meters (GREEN), and 6 meters (BLUE). This could be used to activate the H20 in sequence mechanically.

Red or Green?

MRUs use the same spring as the H20 with a release depth of four meters, using a green top label color. ERUs have a red top label. While the MRU has hydrostatic capabilities, the ERU with a RED/RED top label only will be activated by an electrical signal from an RRS control unit.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What is the lifetime of an H20? Expand

An H20 is a disposable release unit with a guaranteed function of 24 or 30 months of installed life onboard (depending on what is written on the top label). You may store your unit on the shelf, counting back the operational lifetime from the date label.

For example,
A 24-month unit with years 20/21/22/23, may be stored until Dec -21 before installation onboard to obtain the full operational lifetime.

Why is there no "wheel-mark" on my H20 E for EPRIBs? Expand

The "wheel-mark" is the symbol applied to a product approved by the EU's Marine Equipment Directive standards (MED). The directive focuses on HRUs for liferafts, specifying release depth, breaking strength of lashing, and Weak Link™.

The hydrostatic housing in an H20 R and H20 E are the same, but the H20 E is regarded as a component in the free-floating arrangement of Radio Equipment. Therefore, the type of approval (and "wheel-mark") lies with the EPIRB manufacturer.

How do I know which EPIRB bolt/rod is correct for my EPIRB? Expand

For EPIRB-brackets, Hammar's products are regarded as components. In some models, Hammar makes the bolt/rod, but in many models (especially newer), the EPIRB manufacturer holds the design. Hammar's recommendation is to contact the service network of the EPIRB for spares and replacements.

What is the difference between a painter line and bowsing line? Expand

Inflatable liferafts may be equipped with lines for different purposes. The painter line is the raft's activation line connected to the inflation mechanism of the raft. A painter line needs to be connected to a Weak Link™ to activate the raft if a vessel sinks.

The bowsing line is meant to lash the raft to the vessel when embarking and has no function for a liferaft's automatic launch. (For MES, these functions may vary.)

Why is my H20 already marked with expiry date? Expand

The expiry date of an HRU must be marked when installed on a vessel (see instructions on how to mark units further up on this page). A unit that is kept in service past its service life will possibly fail to fulfil SOLAS criteria. During inspections, unmarked units will lead to observations.

To prevent unmarked units from ending up in service, Hammar recommends that release units be marked with an expiry date prior to installation on board.

How do I dispose of my H20 at the end of its service life? Expand

The H20 is made out of glass fiber reinforced nylon. At the end of service life, Hammar recommends disposing of the unit in an environmentally-friendly manner following local regulations.

What is Declaration of Conformity, DoC? Expand

The DOC (Declaration of Conformity) as a document is not connected to the individual Unit but to declare there is a Type Approval connecting the product to a certain chapter of regulation (in the case of HRU, SOLAS; Free Floating arrangement for Life Rafts). The DOC is generic for HRUs leaving Hammar under the validity of the Type Approval. The HRUs for which the DOC is valid are marked with a production date found within the serial number, xxxxx YYMM xxx. This month of the year should be within the dates for the Module B and D in the DOC.

The easiest way to find an HRUs correct DOC  is by scanning the data matrix on the top label using the MarCode app. With this, the DOC will follow the product all the way thru the supply chain.

How can I obtain valid certification for my H20? Expand

Valid certification, such as DoC is accessible through scanning your H20 using the MarCode App. For units manufactured before November 2020, please contact your supplier. You can find the manufacturing date on the top label: xxxxx YYMM xxx.

Can I use my H20 HRU for multiple liferafts? Expand

The design H20 Hydrostatic Release Unit makes the releasing mechanism unaffected by the tension in the white rope sling. This means the H20 will release the lashing even under high tension. The breaking strength of the white rope sling is 15kN. 15kN is the breaking strength of liferaft fittings and painter line system for rafts more than 25 persons set in LSA 4.1.3.2, except for the weak link (LSA 4.1.6).

MGN 343 Clarifies the LSA code further by saying each painter line must be connected to its own weak link.

So even if the breaking strength of the white rope sling, very well, may handle multiple liferafts, the single weak link in each H20 SOLAS standard model means a one-to-one HRU/life raft is recommended.
