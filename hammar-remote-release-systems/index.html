Developed to save lives, optimize work onboard, and improve economy.

REMOTE RELEASE SYSTEMS

Time is precious in an emergency at sea. Each action undertaken to save time will also increase the possibilities to save lives. Hammar's Remote Release Systems (RSS) are designed to enable rapid release of liferafts and other life-saving equipment, with the least amount of effort and risk for the crew. The systems can be operated from the bridge, muster station, or other strategic locations onboard. Hammar's systems are tested and approved to recognized marine standards.

There are two different systems that can be used separately or in various combinations on the same ship:

  • The Electronic Remote Release System
  • The Manual Remote Release System

SAVING LIVES

 

Thanks to a remote triggering system, life-saving and other equipment can be released from the bridge or other strategic locations onboard. This means that in an emergency, you can save precious time. Furthermore, the release mechanism can be deployed irrespective of parameters such as weather, smoke, fire, or degree of list to one side, without the crew having to take any unnecessary risks.

OPTIMIZING WORK ONBOARD

 

Besides saving lives and improving economy, the systems can simplify work onboard in many areas. Closing and opening doors or releasing buoy systems, maintenance equipment, and other heavy loads up to one ton are just a few of many possible applications. You get a head start by planning for Hammar's RRS from the initial design stage. However, the systems can also be retrofitted.

FINANCIAL BENEFITS

 

The systems not only improve safety onboard but also represent a sound economic investment. Liferafts can be positioned in remote locations onboard, and valuable deck surface can be freed for passengers or cargo to increase profitability. Since the systems are easy to handle, you can also minimize both crew and training costs.

ELECTRONIC REMOTE RELEASE SYSTEM

Hammar's Electronic Remote Release Systems (ERRS) use an electrical signal to activate one or more H20 release units. An electronic Control Unit commands the activation of one or several Hammar H20 Electronic Release Units (ERU) with the push of a button. It is also possible to add input sensors such as sensors for water or list angle detection. ERRS has no limitation in the distance between the Control Unit and the Release Unit.

Hammar ERRS is especially suited for:

  • Major systems with many release point liferafts or units
  • Installations more than 50 meters in length

REMOTE OPERATED

 

Most of the HAMMAR ERRS can be operated from several remote release positions by adding one or more remote push buttons (Input Sensor) to the Control Unit. These units can activate H20 ERU (Electronic Release Unit) or relay outputs (depending on configuration). It is thus a very flexible system for the management of safety appliances on board.

SYSTEM CHECKS

 

Most HAMMAR ERRS units automatically perform system checks; which monitors the internal battery, emergency power voltage, ERU circuits, and the wiring to external activation switches. If the system check detects an error, an alarm message will appear on a display or on the LED indicator (depending on unit) and an alarm output is activated.

POWER SUPPLY

 

The ERRS system is powered by the ship's 24 VDC emergency power supply. Most of the ERRS Control Units are also equipped with a back-up battery to enable operation even if the ship´s power supply is down. The systems are designed to be user-friendly, require a minimum of maintenance, and easy to install, even for retrofit.

ERRS - SYSTEM OVERVIEW

Electronic Remote Release System (ERRS) gives you the opportunity to launch safety appliances or an object from a distance in a controlled manner. The activation/launch can be trigged automatically from a sensor or manually from a push button.

For the system to be operational you need some key components:

Electronic Release Units (ERU) Expand

 

The ERU´s mission is to release the object when receiving a signal from the Control Unit. The ERU is often found in a Life-Saving Appliances' lashing arrangement, where it will cut the lashing when activated releasing the LSA. The release unit is replaced after use or after the end of the product life of 24 or 30 months onboard. See our Electronic Release Units here

 

 

Control Units Expand

 

The Control Unit (CU) is the “intelligence” of the ERRS. Depending on the configuration and model; the Control Unit will activate one or more Electronic Release Unit(s) when parameters for activation are met. For example, when the Control Unit receives an incoming signal from a push button or sensor, the Control Unit will process the signal and send out an activation signal to the Electronic Release Unit.

There are Control Units available as both on and off-grid solutions and many of the on-grid solutions are supplied with a back-up battery in case of failure on the 24V Emergency Power Supply.
Examples of Control Units are:

  • Control Panel 2, is our most advanced CU and may control the release of up to 10 Release Units furthermore receive and process signals from 4 different Input Relays. The CP2 can be programmed to let Release Units activated in sequence or one by one
  • List Angle Detection Unit works as a CU or as an Input Relay to a CP2. LAD is an ideal solution for vessels with a risk of capsizing.

 

Installation components Expand

 

Installation Component connects Release Units, Control Units and the optional Input Relays to each other.
The Connector Box (junction box); the terminal inside the box is the interface between the permanently installed cable of the Control Unit and the Release Unit-cable. The Connector Box's enclosure with cable glands protects the wire terminal inside from water ingress (IP66).
The ERRS-cables has to be marine approved and screened to protect the system from external disturbances.

 

Input Sensors Expand

 

 

Input Sensors are optional input connecting to your Control Unit to increased functionality. These relays inform the Control Unit when they detect abnormalities or when a switch has been turned on. Depending on the urgency the Control Unit may proceed with a launch or generate an alarm. 

Examples of Input Sensors are;

  • Extra release push buttons located at the embarkation station,
  • Sensors for water detection in critical spaces 
  • List Angle Detecting (LAD) sending a signal to release when a ship reaches its critical list angle from which it can not recover and subsequently will capsize.
  • Hydrostatic Pressure Switch (HPS), a sensor detecting water pressure

 

 

LIST ANGLE DETECTION

A float-free arrangement with a SOLAS-approved HRU (Hydrostatic Release Unit) will automatically release the liferafts or EPIRBs (Emergency Position Indicating Radio Beacon) at a depth between 1,5 to 4 meters. When a ship capsizes without sinking, there is a major risk that the life-saving appliances are trapped under the craft or never released.

With Hammar's List Angle Detection (LAD) technology, liferafts and EPIRBs can be automatically released at a specified degree of list when a vessel capsizes. The released safety equipment reaches the surface before the ship flips around, significantly reducing the risk of being trapped or entangled in constructions on deck.

MANUAL REMOTE RELEASE SYSTEM

Manual Remote Release Systems (MRRS) activate one or more H20 release units using a vacuum created by a pump. The MRRS is suitable for smaller and less complex arrangements. MRRS are ideal for installations of up to 50 meters in length. Using a hand-operated pneumatic pump, the air inside the tubing is evacuated and creates an under pressure inside the system. The membrane inside the Hammar H20 unit is lifted, and the spring-loaded knife is released.

The Hammar MRRS is especially suited for:

  • Smaller systems with few liferafts or other units
  • Installations up to 50 meters in length

MRRS - SYSTEM OVERVIEW

Manual Remote Release Systems (MRRS) allow you to launch safety appliances or an object from a distance in a controlled manner. You can trigger the activation by using the vacuum pump.

For the system to be operational, you need some key components:

Manual Release Units (MRU) Expand

 

The MRU's mission is to release the object when activated by the pump (control unit). The MRU is often found in a dual arrangement, where it will cut off the lashing in a controlled evacuation. The release unit is replaced after use or after 24 or 30 months onboard.

 

Installation Components Expand

 

Installation components are used to link the pump (control unit) and the MRU (Manual Release Unit). These components are piping (either nylon, but preferably stainless steel) running from the pump at the operator position to the connector U-Console, to which the MRU is connected.

 

Control Units Expand

 

For MRRS (Manual remote Release Systems), the control unit is a pneumatic vacuum pump. The pump will create an under pressure in the pneumatic lines lifting the membrane and the pin holding back the spring-loaded knife in the MRU (Manual Release Unit).

H20 DUAL ASSEMBLY

The H20 Dual Assembly is a combination of release units, including two or more H20 units and/or having two connection points to the deck. The H20 Dual Assembly is used in raft arrangements and RRS (Remote Release Systems) when more than one release criteria are to be fulfilled.

DUAL ASSEMBLY

The H20 Dual Assembly allows for more than one release option and/or redundancy or dual points of release. This is achieved by connecting MRUs (Manual Release Unit) and ERUs (Electronic Release Unit) to an HRU (Hydrostatic Release Unit). This way, you can fulfil SOLAS requirements combined with a remote release.

Dual Assemblies may also be categorized as "for Rafts" or "for MES", where the models for Rafts incorporate a Weak Link (as per SOLAS; 2,2kn).

Dual Assembly for Raft all activate as intended by SOLAS if a vessel sinks before a controlled activation was possible. (H20 - What it Does). The difference between the DA for Raft is what happens when used in a Remote Release System and activated by one. The question to ask when choosing your DA is " What do I want to happen with the Painter Line after a Remote Release?

  • Should it be connected to a Strong Point (15kN)? (the system still works as SOLAS HRU in an uncontrolled situation)

  • Should it be connected to a Weak Link (2,2kN)?

  • Should it be released with the raft?

Installation

Dual Assemblys supplied in various shapes and forms for installation. A few points are essential for the correct function of the unit:

  • In arrangements with two connections to a strong point (deck), these connection points need to be separated by 60 to 120 millimetres.
  • The WeakLink™ may be either a red rope or a WeakLink™ system, depending on the arrangement. Confirm the WeakLink™ is the only thing holding the painter line after an automatic activation.
  • Confirm the joint between the two rope slings is outside the stainless steel ring to avoid pinching upon release.

EXAMPLES OF REMOTE RELEASE SYSTEM APPLICATIONS

LAUNCH OF MARINE EVACUATION SYSTEMS

RRS (Remote Release Systems) are often used in MES (Marine Evacuation Systems) applications for a reliable and accurate launch of different sections in a pre-determined sequence. How and when to initiate each section can be automatically, semi-automatically, or manually controlled.

CONTROLLED RELEASE OF LIFERAFTS

Liferafts can efficiently be launched from racks and cradles, even when they are remotely positioned with no easy access onboard the vessel. These arrangements can optimize the use of valuable deck space and keep crew members safe and out of harm when releasing the liferaft containers.

REMOTE RELEASE OF LIFEBUOYS

A remotely operated lifebuoy on the bridge wing saves precious time when a man-over-board situation occurs, even if it is not mandatory on all ships. The Hammar Lifebuoy Release System rapidly releases the buoy in a controlled manner by pushing an electronic push button.

MAINTENANCE AND TESTING

The maintenance and service of the RRS (Remote Release System) are easy and cost-effective. The systems should be tested and checked regularly, at least once a year. This service can preferably be carried out together with the service of the liferaft. Hammar's RRUs must be replaced after two years of service, or otherwise stated on the product.

ERRS

MAINTENANCE, SERVICE AND TESTING

The Contro Unit should be visually inspected regularly to make sure the system status is OK. Depending on the type of control box, the system status can be shown with LED indicators or on display. (See separate manual)

Note: Make sure the liferafts are secured before any work with the release units takes place to prevent an accidental release of the liferafts or MES (Marine Evacuation System).

Before any electrical installations or upgrades are made, disconnect the external power supply and/or internal battery to the Control Unit.

Annual service

  •  The H20 ERU (Electronic Release Unit) should be replaced every 24 months or otherwise stated on the product.
  •  Backup battery control:
    • (Control Panel 2) If the battery control test indicates insufficient capacity, replace the battery (HM-0725).
    • (BO, LRS, LAD, TiltSensor) If the expiry date printed on the battery label is due, replace the battery (HM-0495).
  • Make a visual inspection of cables, terminals, fittings, and switches. Replace components if necessary. find replacements here.

Testing

Testing of the ERRS should be performed bi-annually, for Systems without a self checking function built in.

  • Disconnect the H20 ERU from the terminal in the connector box.
  • Connect two wires to the Hammar Emulator (HM-0467) and the terminal in the connector box on the positions where the H20 ERU was fitted. Polarity doesn't matter.
  • Turn the emulator on (see ERU Emulator User Manual )
  • Push the release switch, push-button, or activate the sensor in the ERRS. Check that the emulator's LED that flashed green before activation - now shown red - flashes off.
  • Repeat the testing scenario for each H20 ERU position and/or each release switch, push-button, or sensor included in the system.

MRRS

TESTING THE SYSTEM

To test the system, cut the nylon tubing close to the U-Console and remove the remaining part on the nipple. Use a vacuum gauge to test the system and attach this to the free end of the U-Console. A few slow pulls of the pump will give enough vacuum to release.

Note that due to the small-bore diameter of the stainless steel tubing, the vacuum may take a few seconds to build up if the distance from the pump is 20 meters or more. The reading should be at least minus 0,6 bar (-600 millibars) or minus 8,7 PSI. In a properly installed system, the vacuum will be kept for hours.

After successfully testing the system, connect the black nylon tubing from the MRU (Manual Release Unit) to the nipple of the U-Console and lock the tube with the spare nylon sleeve hose. If the nylon tube is too tight to connect to the nipple, it might be gently heated. Make sure the water is not entering the tubing during service.

  • The stainless steel tubing should be rinsed with fresh water regularly.
  • Do not rinse the MRRS (Manual Remote Release System) components or tubing with pressure washers.

Vacuum pump lubricant instruction

  1. Remove the lower bracket and take out the piston rod.
  2. Clean the piston. Do not disassemble the piston.
  3. Apply a layer of new lubricant to the outer surface of the piston.
  4. Carefully insert the piston rod and refit the bracket.
  5. After service, always perform a vacuum test.
  6. Install the safety seal to prevent unauthorised activation.

Note: Lubricate the piston every second year. Always use a lubricant approved by CM Hammar - lubricant TP55 - part no HM-0334 or Molycote 55.

If the vacuum pump does not hold the vacuum after the lubrication service is done, disassemble the stainless steel tubing and ensure that no dirt or burr affect the airflow.

Do not disassemble the non-return valve in the end part. If the failure continues, a new complete part should be ordered.
