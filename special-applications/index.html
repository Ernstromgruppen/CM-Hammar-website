PRODUCTS ADAPTED FOR POLAR CLIMATE

The IMO Polar Code sets increased focus on the capabilities of LSA (Life-Saving Appliances) onboard ships that operate in polar waters. For vessels complying with these standards, Hammar has developed the Polar Box to acquire a habitable climate for our Hammar H20 HRUs (Hydrostatic Release Units) to operate in. The Polar Box is a heated box designed to offer a float-free function to the H20 HRUs down to minus 50 degrees Celsius.

THE POLAR CODE

International Code for Ships Operating in Polar Waters - The Polar Code - is legislation adopted by the IMO under the SOLAS and MARPOL to control design, construction, equipment training, and SAR (Search And Rescue) for ships operating the waters around the North and South Pole.

The code was developed to protect the environment and people in these remote parts of the world. It came into force 1st of January 2017.

THE POLAR BOX

The Polar Box is developed to maintain the operatable environment (-30 to +65 degrees Celcius) for the Hammar H20 HRUs, even in the most severe cold temperature. The Polar Box covers the H20 units in a protective shell equipped with a silicone rubber-coated heater element and a built-in temperature sensor. The heated Polar Box is insulated with PE foam on the inside of the front and backside to reduce heat leakage to the surroundings.

Note that the Polar Box has several air outlet holes at the top of the box to allow quick access to water for the HRU. The heater element and its cable are fully protected against water and humidity.

The HeaterGuard

The heater element is connected to a HeaterGuard temperature control unit placed in a suitable heated space. The HeaterGuard's mission is to maintain a set maximum temperature in the Polar Box. The system has an alarm output that is activated if any of the following fault conditions occur:

  • Power failure on the power supply or blown fuse
  • Low temperature in the heater or open sensor circuit
  • High temperature in the heater or short circuit in the sensor circuit
  • Heater not responding or heater circuit broken

How to choose a Polar Box

Hammar's Polar Box is available in a range of varieties:

  • Heated for one or two H20 units

If your liferaft is equipped with a heating blanket, you may choose an option with one H20 unit cutting the cable and one H20 unit cutting the lashing.

  • HRUs or ERUs

The Polar Box can be arranged around the ERU (Electronic Release Unit) in an ERRS (Electronic Remote Release System) or with an HRU (Hydrostatic Release Unit).

OUR EX-PRODUCTS

There are two different schemes for certifying equipment intended for use in potentially explosive atmospheres: IECEx and ATEX. Schemes are generally incorporated by classification societies' (such as DNVGL and Bureau Veritas) class rules making them mandatory to classed vessels. Flag state, cargo terminals, and cargo owners may set up their own regulations for potentially hazardous environments. 

IECEx

The most common is the voluntary scheme for harmonization under IEC (International Electrotechnical Commission), the IECEx. Schemes are generally incorporated by classification societies' (such as DNVGL and Bureau Veritas) class rules making them mandatory to classed vessels. Flag-state, cargo terminals, and cargo owners may set up their own regulations for potentially hazardous environments. 

The potentially hazardous environments are divided into zones that determine the level of protection needed for the respective zone's equipment.

  • Zone 0 - Area in which an explosive gas atmosphere is present continuously or is present for long periods.
  • Zone 1 - Area in which an explosive gas atmosphere is likely to occur in normal operation.
  • Zone 2 - Area in which an explosive gas atmosphere is not likely to occur in normal operation. If it does, it is likely to do so only infrequently and will only exist for a short period. (IEC:1999(E)ELECTRICAL INSTALLATIONS IN SHIPS –Part 502: Tankers – Special features)

ATEX

The EU Directive 2014/34/EU, formally know as ATEX, does not apply to seagoing vessels and mobile offshore units together with equipment on board such as vessels or units. Ports, terminals, and flag states in the European Union are obliged to follow these regulations, which is why it is also applied to ships.

For applications in these potentially hazardous environments, Hammar has developed and certified a range of products approved to ATEX and IECEx standards.
